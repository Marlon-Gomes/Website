Past teaching and outreach activities

I've been teaching at Stony Brook since the Fall of 2014. During this time, I have been a grader, taught recitations, and have been Instructor for Summer courses.

Previously I worked as a coach for Middle School and High School Math Olympiads (2011-2014) in various private schools in my hometown (Fortaleza, Brazil). I was also involved in a project of the local goverment of the city of Varzea Alegre, Brazil, to bring Math Olympiad Education to underpriviledged students from adjacent rural areas.

At the College level, I had a short experience before coming to Stony Brook, as a Substitute Professor at Universidade da Integração Internacional da Lusofonia Afro-Brasileira (Redenção, Brazil) in 2012, as well as at my alma mater, Universidade Federal do Ceará (Fortaleza, Brazil), between 2013 and 2014.

I also participated in outreach activities involving High School students, through the Math in Jeans (2017) and Summer Math Camp (2016 to 2019) programs, two initiatives of the Institute for STEM Education.
